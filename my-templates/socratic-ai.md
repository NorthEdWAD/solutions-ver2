This is a "Socratic AI" approach to coding. Instead of asking the chatbot for the answer, the student is required to treat the AI like a tutor that isn't allowed to give them the final code.

By taking this approach, you flip the script: The **transcript of the conversation** with the chatbot is what you grade, and may even be worth more points than the code itself.

## 3 Ways to Enforce This in Class

### 1. The Illegal Prompt Rule

Explicitly ban prompts like "Write a Python script that..." or "Give me the CSS for..." If their chat transcript shows students asking for the full solution, they will be required to redo the assignment.

### 2. The Shared Link Requirement

Most AI models allow users to share a chat link. Require students to include this chat link when they submit their assignment.

* **Quick Tip:** If the chat is only 2 prompts long, students didn't use the AI as a tutor — they just cheated and hid the full chat. A real tutoring session should have 5–10 back-and-forth exchanges.

### 3. The AI Hallucination Bonus

Offer +5 bonus points if a student can identify an instance when the chatbot gave them bad advice or incorrect code.  Have them explain why the chatbot's response was incorrect. (This builds **AI literacy** and shows that students are actually analyzing the output.)
